1. Headlines lie by omission. Public line items (Olympics €6.6bn; France 2030 tens of billions; EDF provisions €68.8bn) are real but incomplete. That gap weakens claims that “nuclear has near-zero lifecycle emissions” unless you clearly define system boundaries.


4. Policy implication: the currently reported budgets downplay risk. Public decision-making (build new reactors, fund Cigéo, host mega-events) routinely ignores cross-budget externalities (defence, long-term monitoring, contingency), thus socialising downside risk while private actors or project promoters get the upside narrative.
